It seems the two words, verification and validation, are often used interchangeably, to mean something like "to assess the accuracy of".

The American Heritage Dictionary of the English Language defines these two words as follows:

val.i.date - 1. To declare or make legally valid.  2. To mark with an indication of official sanction.  3. To substantiate; verify.

ver.i.fy - 1. To prove the truth of by the presentation of evidence or testimony; substantiate. 2. To determine or test the truth or accuracy of, as by comparison, investigation, or reference: "Findings are not accepted by scientists unless they can be verified." (Norman L. Munn)
